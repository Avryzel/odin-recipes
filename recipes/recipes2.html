Rendang

Description

Rendang is a rich and flavorful Indonesian dish made from slow-cooked beef simmered in coconut milk and a mixture of aromatic spices. The cooking process, which can take several hours, allows the meat to become tender and the sauce to thicken and caramelize, resulting in a dark, intense, and complex flavor. Traditionally served at festive occasions and celebrations, rendang is beloved for its deep, savory taste and its ability to be preserved for a long time without refrigeration.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef (preferably from the shank), cut into cubes
  • 400 ml coconut milk
  • 2 cups water
  • 4 kaffir lime leaves
  • 2 turmeric leaves, tied in a knot
  • 2 lemongrass stalks, smashed
  • 2 cm galangal, smashed
  • 2 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt to taste

Spice Paste

  • 10 shallots
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 5 cm ginger
  • 5 cm galangal
  • 5 cm turmeric
  • 6 red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 5 candlenuts

How To Make

  1. Blend the shallots, garlic, ginger, galangal, turmeric, red chilies, and candlenuts into a smooth paste. You may need to add a little water to help the blending process.

  2. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the spice paste and cook until fragrant and the oil starts to separate from the paste, about 5-10 minutes.

  3. Add the beef cubes to the pot and cook until the beef is browned on all sides.

  4. Pour in the coconut milk and water. Add the kaffir lime leaves, turmeric leaves, lemongrass, and galangal. Stir well to combine.

  5. ring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer uncovered. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Cook until the beef is tender and the sauce has thickened and darkened, about 2-3 hours.

  6. Add the tamarind paste, sugar, and salt to taste. Continue to cook until the oil separates from the sauce, and the beef is tender and caramelized.

  7. Remove the kaffir lime leaves, turmeric leaves, lemongrass, and galangal. Serve the rendang with steamed rice.
