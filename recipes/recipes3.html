Honey Butter Chicken

Description

Honey Butter Chicken is a delightful dish featuring crispy fried chicken pieces coated in a rich and flavorful sauce made from honey, butter, soy sauce, and garlic. The combination of sweet and savory flavors, along with a hint of garlic, makes this dish irresistibly delicious. Perfectly paired with steamed rice or your favorite side, Honey Butter Chicken is a crowd-pleaser for any meal.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 eggs
  • Oil for frying

Honey Butter Sauce

  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar or rice vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced

How To Make

  1. In a bowl, mix the flour, cornstarch, salt, black pepper, and garlic powder.

  2. In another bowl, beat the eggs.

  3. Dip each piece of chicken into the egg, then coat with the flour mixture. Set aside.

  4. Heat oil in a deep pan or fryer to 175°C (350°F). Fry the chicken pieces in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes per batch. Remove and drain on paper towels.

  5. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.

  6. Add the honey, soy sauce, apple cider vinegar, and red pepper flakes (if using). Stir to combine and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes until slightly thickened.

  7. Add the fried chicken pieces to the skillet with the honey butter sauce. Toss to coat the chicken evenly with the sauce.

  8. Serve the honey butter chicken hot, garnished with sesame seeds and chopped green onions if desired. Enjoy with steamed rice or your favorite side dish.
